1 What is the difference between Air quality and Air quality index? +
“Air Quality” represents the absolute nature of air pollution. “Air Quality Index” is an extract from air quality as a number to simplify the pollution alert system and to support public awareness.
2 What is the meaning of AQI=500 or AQI=999 ? +
If absolute PM2.5 concentration is 500 or 1000 or 3000 g/m3, the AQI index will show 500 or 999, depending on the selected scale.
3 Diseases caused by PM2.5? +
PM2.5 pollution is directly linked to cause or exacerbate asthma, collapsed lung, chronic bronchitis, pneumonia, blocked lung artery, lung cancer, complications during pregnancy, early childhood brain function impairmentand.

6 Why (WQI) shows negative correlation with pH value and positive with others parameters? +
As pH value decreases, water tends to become more acidic, making it less suitable for consumption or aquatic life. While parameters like DO and TDS have a positive correlation with the WQI as they indicate healthier water conditions.
7 What is ORP in water? +
Oxidation-reduction potential (ORP) measures the ability of a lake or river to cleanse itself or break down waste products, such as contaminants and dead plants and animals. When the ORP value is high, there is lots of oxygen present in the water.
8 What are the 4 types of water quality? +
Water quality can be classified into four types—potable water, palatable water, contaminated (polluted) water, and infected water.
9 Why is water polar? +
Water molecule has two poles - a positive charge on the hydrogen pole (side) and a negative charge on the oxygen pole (side). We say that the water molecule is electrically polar.
10 What is the World Water Quality Alliance and what is its role? +
The World Water Quality Alliance (WWQA), represents a voluntary and flexible global multi-stakeholders network that advocates the central role of freshwater quality.
